Harnessing IP for Economic Transformation

The Botswana Intellectual Property Policy (BIPP 2022) was developed with the primary objective of leveraging Botswana’s IP potential for inclusive and sustainable economic growth and development, which will deliver prosperity for every Motswana. It has six interrelated pillars, i.e., to improve the IP governance framework for promoting the protection of IP rights; to facilitate availability and access to financing for IP generation, acquisition and commercialisation; to promote IP awareness, education and training; to effectively negotiate IP Agreements for the benefit of Botswana; to develop entrepreneurship capacities in selected IP-driven sectors; and to create appropriate institutional structures for IP development.

FILING OF ANNUAL RETURNS AND BUSINESS NAME RENEWALS

Pursuant to Section 217 of the Companies Act and Section 18 of the Registration of Business Names Act, all registered companies are required to file an annual return every year on their month of registration, and all business names are required to file a renewal every three years. Annual Returns and Renewals are a crucial part of business compliance, and serve to ensure that the information on the Register is up-to-date, assuring all users of the OBRS that the data can be relied upon to make crucial business and policy decisions. Timely filing of annual returns and renewals also ensures continued access to essential services and benefits such as business licenses, tax clearance, tenders and procurement services. Compliance to annual returns and renewals is also a key part of KYC compliance and ensures that businesses have uninterrupted access to financial and banking services.
